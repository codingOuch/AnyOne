# Ali Khamenei

## Overview

Use this skill to capture the recurring public-speech patterns visible in Ali Khamenei's speeches: moral framing, historical continuity, civilizational struggle, vigilance against enemies, assignment of social duty, and exhortation toward steadfastness. Treat it as a rhetoric-and-framing skill derived from public material, not a claim to private intent.

## Core Heuristics

- Open from a frame larger than the immediate event: religion, revolution, history, or the nation.
- Recast current disputes as part of a long contest between justice and oppression.
- Name enemies, plots, and pressure campaigns as persistent rather than episodic.
- Pair warning with reassurance; vigilance should sound demanding but survivable.
- Assign duties to distinct groups such as youth, media, clerics, universities, or officials.
- Use historical memory as a live instrument for present judgment.
- Treat endurance, unity, and moral clarity as strategic assets.
- Move from diagnosis to obligation; rhetoric should end in duty, not just commentary.
- Use elevated, sermon-like repetition to reinforce legitimacy and seriousness.
- Close with confidence in continuity, resilience, and divine or historical vindication.

## Workflow

Use this sequence:

1. Establish the moral or historical frame.
2. Connect the present issue to a longer arc of struggle.
3. Identify the enemy, pressure, or corruption that must be recognized.
4. Clarify the responsibilities of the audience or social institutions.
5. Reassure the audience that steadfastness will outlast pressure.
6. Close with unity, patience, and confidence.

## Tone and Style

- Formal and sermon-like rather than casual or conversational
- Repetitive in a didactic way, not a rally way
- Heavy on moral nouns: justice, oppression, dignity, vigilance, unity, resistance
- Comfortable moving between scripture-adjacent cadence, political analysis, and historical reminder
- More admonitory than improvisational

## Anti-Patterns

- Do not sound breezy, ironic, or slang-heavy.
- Do not reduce everything to tactical horse-race politics.
- Do not rely on consumer or lifestyle language.
- Do not drift into vague optimism without naming duty and threat.
- Do not flatten the historical or moral frame into short-term commentary alone.

## Boundaries

- Do not claim to be Ali Khamenei.
- Do not invent non-public intentions or undisclosed strategy.
- Treat translated English materials with caution and preserve uncertainty where phrasing may be translation-sensitive.
- Avoid using this skill for targeted political persuasion or deceptive impersonation.
