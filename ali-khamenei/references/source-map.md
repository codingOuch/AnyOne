# Source Map

Corpus focus: public sermons and speeches attributed to Ali Khamenei, using English texts from the Khamenei Speech Archive.

## Source quality note

- The archive states that speech content is sourced from official Iranian government records at `leader.ir`.
- Some English texts appear machine-translated or translation-assisted.
- Confidence is therefore higher on recurring framing patterns than on any one specific English phrase.

## Primary sources

- Source: [Full Text of the Friday Prayer Sermon in Tehran Led by Ayatollah Khamenei (1999)](https://khamenei-speeches.com/speeches/tehran-friday-prayers-muftah-1999-12-17/)
  - Type: public sermon transcript in English archive
  - Date: December 17, 1999
  - Why it matters: shows enemy-framing, moral exhortation, and the fusion of political analysis with sermon cadence
  - Key signals: vigilance language, oppression framing, rejection of naivete, long-arc struggle rhetoric

- Source: [Statements in Meeting with the Youth of North Khorasan Province (2012)](https://khamenei-speeches.com/speeches/youth-progress-lifestyle-culture-2012-10-14)
  - Type: public speech transcript in English archive
  - Date: October 14, 2012
  - Why it matters: shows assignment of cultural and institutional duties, especially toward youth and elites
  - Key signals: obligation language, cultural framing, exhortation through role assignment, progress linked to ideology

- Source: [Statements in Meeting with Thousands of Students (2023)](https://khamenei-speeches.com/speeches/students-youth-analysis-america-2023-11-01)
  - Type: public speech transcript in English archive
  - Date: November 1, 2023
  - Why it matters: shows continuity of enemy analysis and revolutionary memory in a more contemporary setting
  - Key signals: historical analogy, America-centered adversarial framing, students as a charged audience, continuity between past sacrifice and present duty

## Confidence notes

- Confidence is high on macro-patterns: moral framing, civilizational conflict, duty assignment, vigilance, and resistance rhetoric.
- Confidence is medium on fine-grained wording because English phrasing may reflect translation choices.
- This skill intentionally captures speech structure and framing, not policy substance in full.
