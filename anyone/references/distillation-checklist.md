# Distillation Checklist

Use this checklist before writing the derived skill.

## Evidence threshold

Try to collect at least:

- 3 strong first-party sources, or
- 2 strong first-party sources plus multiple corroborating secondary sources

Prefer breadth across formats. A person's essays plus interviews usually reveal more than a single viral clip.

## Source rubric

For each source, record:

- title or identifier
- source type
- approximate date
- why it matters
- confidence level: high, medium, or low

Prefer sources that show repeated behavior over sources that only show polished marketing.

## Extraction lenses

Extract notes under these headings:

### 1. What does this person optimize for?

Look for explicit goals, repeated trade-offs, and what they praise in others.

### 2. How does this person make decisions?

Look for heuristics, questions, checklists, and favored comparisons.

### 3. What does this person notice that others ignore?

Look for unusual distinctions, taste signals, framing habits, and diagnostic moves.

### 4. How does this person communicate?

Capture sentence length, density, use of examples, use of metaphor, and level of directness.

### 5. How does this person structure work?

Look for recurring loops such as:

- research -> synthesis -> critique -> revision
- prototype -> test -> simplify -> ship
- observe -> decompose -> rank -> decide

### 6. What does this person reject?

Look for anti-patterns, taboos, recurring complaints, and failure modes they warn against.

## Stable vs time-sensitive

Split findings into:

- Stable: principles, heuristics, taste, workflow habits, rhetorical patterns
- Time-sensitive: company roles, current projects, current market views, dated references

Move time-sensitive material into references unless it is necessary for the derived skill to function.

## Red flags

Do not overfit to:

- a single interview
- a parody or meme version of the person
- fan summaries that copy each other
- style markers without decision logic
- dated opinions presented as universal rules

## Minimum useful output

A derived skill is usually good enough when it includes:

- 5 to 10 strong heuristics
- 3 to 5 signature workflow patterns
- 3 to 5 anti-patterns
- concise tone rules backed by examples
- explicit uncertainty notes for weak claims
