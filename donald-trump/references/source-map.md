# Source Map

Corpus focus: formal public addresses and major speeches delivered by Donald Trump in office.

## Primary sources

- Source: [Remarks by President Trump in Joint Address to Congress](https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/briefings-statements/remarks-president-trump-joint-address-congress/)
  - Type: official White House transcript
  - Date: February 28, 2017
  - Why it matters: shows structured national-address mode rather than rally improvisation
  - Key signals: direct problem statements, simple contrast pairs, repetitive issue framing, patriotic closure

- Source: [Remarks by President Trump to the People of Poland](https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/briefings-statements/remarks-president-trump-people-poland/)
  - Type: official White House speech transcript
  - Date: July 6, 2017
  - Why it matters: shows civilizational framing, historical storytelling, and repeated value language
  - Key signals: people-versus-threat framing, rhetorical repetition, praise-then-warning structure, civilization vocabulary

- Source: [Farewell Address](https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/farewell-address/)
  - Type: official White House address transcript
  - Date: January 19, 2021
  - Why it matters: shows reflective mode while preserving core speech habits
  - Key signals: legacy scorekeeping, praise for ordinary Americans, quick pivot from grievance to pride, certainty-heavy endings

## Confidence notes

- Confidence is high on recurring rhetorical markers such as repetition, blunt contrast, restoration framing, and oral cadence.
- Confidence is medium on broader decision-making heuristics because this sample is speech-centered rather than management-centered.
- This skill intentionally captures public-address style, not governing substance or complete worldview.
