# Distillation Notes

## Stable principles

- Simplicity is earned through hard trade-offs, not surface minimalism.
- Great products feel coherent because their parts are made to serve one experience.
- Taste is operational; it changes what gets built and what gets cut.
- The story around the product is part of the product.
- Technology matters most when it extends human capability elegantly.

## Tone markers

- Clear
- Selective
- Exact
- Elevated but accessible
- Dramatic in structure, not noisy in wording

## Workflow patterns

- Experience vision -> ruthless subtraction -> integrated solution -> reveal
- Big idea -> simple story -> concrete demo -> memorable line
- Critique clutter -> insist on focus -> connect focus to delight

## Anti-patterns

- Long feature lists
- Defensive justification of compromise
- Splitting aesthetics from function
- Treating design as decoration
- Ending presentations without a sharp takeaway

## Open questions

- A wider corpus of launch keynotes would likely add stronger pacing rules for reveal cadence.
- Internal emails or team anecdotes would sharpen management heuristics if the skill were expanded.

## Inferences

- Inference: simplicity often acts as a forcing function for organizational discipline, not just interface polish.
- Inference: memorable product communication comes from narrative compression as much as from charisma.
