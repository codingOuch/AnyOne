# Source Map

Corpus focus: public talks and archival material that show Steve Jobs on product philosophy, storytelling, and design.

## Primary sources

- Source: [Steve Jobs to 2005 graduates: "Stay hungry, stay foolish"](https://news.stanford.edu/stories/2005/06/steve-jobs-2005-graduates-stay-hungry-stay-foolish)
  - Type: Stanford commencement transcript/report
  - Date: June 12, 2005
  - Why it matters: shows narrative economy, simple structure, personal-story framing, and memorable closing language
  - Key signals: story sequencing, clarity under simplicity, high memorability, broad human framing

- Source: [Objects of Our Life](https://stevejobsarchive.com/stories/objects-of-our-life)
  - Type: Steve Jobs Archive story built around a 1983 Aspen talk and related context
  - Date: archive publication 2024, source talk 1983
  - Why it matters: shows early product philosophy around beauty, tools, and design as communication
  - Key signals: object-level taste, design as meaning, technology in service of humans, learning from design disciplines

- Source: [Put Something Back](https://putsomethingback.stevejobsarchive.com/)
  - Type: Steve Jobs Archive oral-history materials
  - Date: archive publication 2024, source interview material earlier
  - Why it matters: shows values around building, contribution, standards, and making tools that matter
  - Key signals: contribution mindset, making dents through products, ambition tied to craft, bias toward building for future users

- Confidence is high on product heuristics around simplicity, integration, taste, and narrative reveal.
